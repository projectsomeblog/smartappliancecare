Common Fridge Problems They Fix

  • Fridge not cooling properly: Often caused by a faulty compressor, thermostat, or refrigerant leak.
  • Freezer icing or over-freezing: Usually linked to defrost system failures or airflow issues.
  • Water leaking inside or underneath the fridge: Typically due to a clogged defrost drain or cracked water line.
  • Unusual noises: Clicking, buzzing, or rattling sounds can indicate fan, motor, or compressor problems.
  • Damaged door seals (gaskets): Worn seals allow cold air to escape, forcing your fridge to work harder.
  • Electrical or control panel faults: Malfunctioning digital displays, sensors, or circuit boards.

Simple Maintenance Tips to Keep Your Fridge Running Longer

  • Clean condenser coils every six months to remove dust and debris.
  • Inspect and clean door seals regularly to maintain an airtight fit.
  • Avoid overloading your fridge; allow proper airflow inside.
  • Defrost the freezer when ice builds up more than 5 mm thick.
  • Keep the appliance level so the doors seal properly.
  • Position your fridge away from direct sunlight or heat sources.

These simple steps can help prevent overheating, poor cooling performance, and unnecessary energy consumption.
